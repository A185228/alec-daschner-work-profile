## 📚 Writing & Documentation Resources

### Style & Writing Standards
- **Plain Language Principles** — Write at 8th-grade reading level
- **KISS Principle** — Keep It Simple, Stupid
- **Accessibility Standards** — WCAG 2.1 guidelines for accessible content
- **Information Architecture** — Organized, logical content structure
- **Active Voice** — Clearer, more direct communication

### Clear Writing Best Practices

**Structure & Organization:**
1. Clear purpose statement or introduction
2. Logical organization (chronological, importance, or category-based)
3. Consistent formatting and hierarchy
4. Helpful examples and illustrations
5. Easy-to-find information

**Language & Clarity:**
1. Use simple, common words
2. Keep sentences short (15-20 words average)
3. One main idea per paragraph
4. Use active voice
5. Avoid unnecessary jargon or explain it
6. Use transitions between ideas

**Content Quality:**
- Accurate and verified information
- Practical, actionable content
- Helpful examples
- Clear instructions
- Consistent terminology
- Professional tone

### Documentation Types

- **User Guides** — How to use a product or system
- **Procedures** — Step-by-step instructions
- **Policies** — Rules and standards
- **FAQs** — Frequently asked questions
- **Technical Documentation** — System specifications
- **Quick Reference Guides** — Condensed information

## 🛡️ Safety & Risk Management Resources

### Risk Assessment Framework

**Risk Scoring Methodology:**
- Probability Scale: 1-5 (Very unlikely to Very likely)
- Impact Scale: 1-5 (Negligible to Critical)
- Risk Score = Probability × Impact
- Prioritize by risk score

**Risk Management Process:**
1. **Identify** — What could go wrong?
2. **Analyze** — How likely? What's the impact?
3. **Prioritize** — Which risks matter most?
4. **Mitigate** — How do we reduce the risk?
5. **Implement** — Put controls in place
6. **Monitor** — Is it working?
7. **Review** — Update regularly

### Incident Investigation

**Incident Investigation Steps:**
1. Document incident immediately
2. Interview participants and witnesses
3. Gather evidence and documentation
4. Conduct root cause analysis
5. Identify contributing factors
6. Develop corrective actions
7. Implement improvements
8. Verify effectiveness

**Root Cause Analysis Techniques:**
- Five Why Analysis — Ask "why" 5 times
- Fishbone Diagram — Identify contributing factors
- Timeline Analysis — Sequence of events
- Fault Tree Analysis — Multiple causation paths

### Safety Culture Elements

- Leadership commitment and modeling
- Clear policies and procedures
- Regular training and competency
- Open communication and reporting
- Incident investigation and learning
- Continuous improvement mindset
- Accountability and ownership
- Resource allocation

## ✅ Compliance & Standards Resources

### Compliance Framework Approach

**8-Step Compliance Process:**
1. **Understand Requirements** — What standards/regulations apply?
2. **Gap Analysis** — Where are you now vs. required?
3. **Policy Development** — Create required policies
4. **Procedure Development** — Document how things are done
5. **Control Implementation** — Put systems in place
6. **Staff Training** — Ensure understanding
7. **Monitoring & Testing** — Regular verification
8. **Documentation & Evidence** — Maintain audit trail

### Common Standards & Frameworks

**Quality Standards:**
- ISO 9001 — Quality Management Systems
- ISO 27001 — Information Security Management
- SOC 2 — Security, Availability, Processing Integrity

**Regulatory Standards:**
- GDPR — Data Protection (EU)
- HIPAA — Healthcare Privacy (US)
- PCI DSS — Payment Card Security
- OSHA — Occupational Safety (US)

**Governance Frameworks:**
- COSO — Internal Controls and Risk
- COBIT — IT Governance
- ITIL — IT Service Management

### Audit Readiness

**Preparation Steps:**
1. Document all procedures and policies
2. Create evidence trail and records
3. Identify and remediate gaps
4. Train staff on audit process
5. Organize documentation
6. Establish audit timeline

**Audit Evidence:**
- Documented policies and procedures
- Records of training and competency
- Monitoring and test results
- Incident reports and investigations
- Management reviews
- Corrective action records

## 📊 Administrative Support Resources

### Process Improvement Techniques

**Process Mapping:**
1. List all steps in current process
2. Identify who does each step
3. Estimate time required
4. Identify decision points
5. Find bottlenecks and delays
6. Identify redundant steps

**Process Optimization:**
1. Eliminate unnecessary steps
2. Combine related activities
3. Automate where possible
4. Clarify responsibilities
5. Establish clear handoffs
6. Measure improvement

**Project Management Essentials:**
- Clear scope and objectives
- Defined timeline and milestones
- Resource planning and allocation
- Risk identification and management
- Regular status reporting
